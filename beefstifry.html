Beef Stir-Fry

A quick and colorful dish loaded with tender beef strips and crisp vegetables

Introduction

This beef stir-fry is fast, delicious, and perfect for busy weeknights. Tender beef slices are tossed with colorful vegetables in a savory soy-based sauce, served hot over rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef sirloin or flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch (for coating beef)
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Toss beef slices with cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok or large pan. Stir-fry beef for 2–3 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining oil, then sauté garlic, onion, and ginger for 1 minute.
  4. Add bell peppers and carrot. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until slightly tender but still crisp.
  5. Return beef to the pan. Add soy sauce and oyster sauce. Stir well until everything is coated and heated through.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Remove from heat.
Tip: Slice the beef thinly against the grain for maximum tenderness.

Serving Suggestion

Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds or spring onions for extra flavor.
