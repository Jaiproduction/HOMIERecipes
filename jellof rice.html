Jollof Rice

West African Classic — smoky, spicy & perfect for family meals

Introduction

A smoky, spicy, and rich one-pot rice dish loved across West Africa. Perfect for parties or family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 3 medium tomatoes
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (or less for mild heat)
  • 1 medium onion (half blended, half sliced)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, peppers, and half the onion into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a pot, add sliced onions, fry until golden.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, fry for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add blended mixture, thyme, curry, bay leaves, and simmer until sauce thickens.
  5. Add rice, chicken stock, and salt. Stir, cover with foil and lid to trap steam.
  6. Cook on low heat for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally until rice is fluffy and smoky.
Tip: For the famous “party Jollof” smoky flavor, let the rice steam gently until the bottom layer toasts slightly.

Serving Suggestion

Best served with fried plantain, grilled chicken, or a fresh salad.
