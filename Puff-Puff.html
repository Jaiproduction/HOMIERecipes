Puff-Puff (Fried Dough Balls)

A sweet, fluffy West African snack — golden, soft, and irresistible

Introduction

Puff-puff is a popular West African street food made from deep-fried dough. It’s sweet, fluffy, and perfect as a snack, dessert, or party treat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1¼ cups warm water
  • ½ tsp nutmeg (optional)
  • Vegetable oil (for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, yeast, salt, and nutmeg.
  2. Gradually add warm water and stir until you get a smooth, thick batter.
  3. Cover the bowl with a cloth and let it rise for 45–60 minutes until doubled in size.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  5. Using your hands or a spoon, scoop small portions of the dough into the oil.
  6. Fry until puff-puffs are golden brown, turning occasionally for even cooking.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels before serving.
Tip: For extra flavor, roll puff-puff in sugar or drizzle with honey after frying.

Serving Suggestion

Enjoy as a snack with tea or juice, or serve at parties alongside savory small chops.
