Grilled Fish with Plantains

A flavorful West African favorite — smoky grilled fish paired with sweet fried plantains

Introduction

This dish combines smoky, marinated grilled fish with the sweetness of golden fried plantains. Popular in West Africa, it’s simple, nutritious, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 whole fish (tilapia, snapper, or mackerel), cleaned
  • 3 ripe plantains
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, blended
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp paprika or chili powder
  • 1 tsp ginger powder
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 fresh lemon (juice)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil (for frying plantains)

Instructions

  1. Make a marinade with onion, garlic, oil, paprika, ginger, thyme, curry, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Score the fish with shallow cuts and rub the marinade all over, including inside. Let sit 30 minutes.
  3. Grill the fish over medium heat (charcoal or oven grill) for 20–25 minutes, turning occasionally, until cooked and slightly charred.
  4. Meanwhile, peel and slice plantains diagonally. Heat oil in a pan and fry plantains until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Drain plantains on paper towels and serve alongside the grilled fish.
Tip: For an authentic smoky taste, grill over charcoal. You can also roast in the oven if charcoal grilling isn’t possible.

Serving Suggestion

Best served hot with a side of pepper sauce, coleslaw, or fresh vegetable salad.
